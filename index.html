Research Interests and Publications

Die Ros' ist ohn' Warum; sie blühet, weil sie blühet
Sie acht' nicht ihrer selbst, fragt nicht, ob man sie siehet. (Silesius, 1657)

Operations research is my field of activity and my research is mainly concerned with combinatorial algorithms with applications to scheduling, logistics, manufacturing and transportation systems.

My work included: Design and analysis of decision models for resource allocation, computational complexity characterisation, design of exact and approximate algorithms for multi-agent and nonlinear combinatorial optimization problems; design and implementation of simulation models for control policies evaluation in complex systems. Bio

  • Since Feb. 2025 Full professor at Dipartimento di Ingegneria Civile e Ingegneria Informatica, Università di Roma Tor Vergata.
  • Dec. 2010 - Jan. 2025 Associate professor at Dipartimento di Ingegneria Civile e Ingegneria Informatica (until May, 2012, Dip. di Informatica, Sistemi e Produzione), Università di Roma Tor Vergata.
  • Nov. 2003 - Dec. 2010 Assistant professor at Dipartimento di Ingegneria dell'Impresa, Università di Roma Tor Vergata.
  • Nov. 1999 - Nov. 2003 Assistant professor at Dipartimento di Informatica, Sistemi e Produzione, Università di Roma Tor Vergata.
  • 2004 (May-Jul.) Visiting Faculty at ATLAS Research Center, The University of Arizona, Tucson, Arizona.
  • 1997-2000 Research fellow at Dipartimento di Informatica e Automazione, Università degli Studi Roma Tre.
  • 1997 (Apr.-Jul.) Visiting Scholar at the System and Industrial Engineering Dept., The University of Arizona, Tucson, Arizona (supervisor Prof. Pitu B. Mirchandani).
  • 1996 Research fellow at the CNR (National Research Council).
  • 1993-1996 PhD program in Operations Research at Università di Roma La Sapienza. (Ph.Doctor on July 1997.)
  • 1993 "Laurea" degree in Electronic Engineering with full marks at Università di Roma La Sapienza.
